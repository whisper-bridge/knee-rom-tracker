Getting Started

4 Steps to Tracking

From unboxing to your first accurate measurement in under 2 minutes.

1

Power & Position

Connect CPX to power via USB or battery. The device lights up YELLOW, indicating ready for calibration. Mount on the lateral side of your knee using an elastic strap.

2

Calibrate

Sit with knee fully straight (full extension). Press Button A. Blue light with cyan dots indicates calibration in progress. Hold still for 2 seconds.

3

Start Exercise

Green flash and beep means ready! Begin your exercises. The LED ring shows real-time angle with color coding: Green (0-45°), Yellow (45-90°), Red (90-135°).

4

Monitor Progress

Connect USB to view detailed dashboard data. Track ROM trends, repetition counts, and quality scores. Press Button B to reset counter between sets.

Controls

Button Functions

Two buttons, complete control over your tracking session.

A

Button A — Calibrate

Primary Function: Start calibration sequence

When to use: Every time you put the device on, or if you notice drift during exercise

What it does: Zeros all angles, calculates gyro bias, establishes your full extension as 0°

Hold knee straight → Press A → Wait for green
B

Button B — Reset Counter

Primary Function: Reset repetition and ROM tracking

When to use: Between exercise sets, or starting a new exercise type

What it does: Clears max flexion/extension values, keeps calibration intact

Press B anytime → White flash → Ready for new set
Critical Setup

Proper Placement

Correct positioning is essential for accurate measurements.

Important: Incorrect placement is the #1 cause of inaccurate readings. Follow these guidelines carefully for clinical-grade results.

Location: Lateral Knee Joint

Place the CPX on the outside (lateral side) of your knee, directly over the joint line. Avoid muscle or soft tissue—sensor must track bone movement.

Orientation: USB Port Up

The USB port should face upward toward your hip. LED ring should be visible from the side when standing. This ensures correct axis alignment.

Secure Attachment

Use an elastic strap with silicone grip. Device should not move independently of your knee. Strap should be snug but not restrict blood flow.

Support

Troubleshooting Guide

Common issues and quick solutions to keep you tracking.

Red Light Won't Go Away

Cause: Sensor error or poor placement
Fix: Check CPX is right-side up (USB up). Reposition on bony part of knee. Restart device if persistent.

Angles Seem Wrong

Cause: Calibration done with bent knee
Fix: Straighten knee fully, press Button A to recalibrate. Ensure 0° = full extension reference.

Readings Drift Over Time

Cause: Normal gyroscope drift
Fix: Hold knee still for 1 second—device auto-corrects. Or press Button A to recalibrate.

Purple Dot Appears

Cause: Low data quality (sensor moving too much)
Fix: Tighten strap, ensure CPX moves with knee not skin. Check for loose connections.

No Beeps During Exercise

Cause: Not completing full range of motion
Fix: Ensure you go from full extension (<10°) to at least 30° flexion for rep to count.

Device Not Powering On

Cause: Battery depleted or USB issue
Fix: Try different USB cable/port. If using battery, ensure it's charged (3.7V LiPo).
